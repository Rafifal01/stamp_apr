Key considerations

✓
55% COGS is your floor, not your ceiling. v2 actual COGS data pointed to ~46–47%. If operations track there, net profit per card rises to ~Rp 150k and break-even drops to ~117 cards. Every percentage point below 55% goes straight to the bottom line.
▲
Reward food COGS is your biggest ongoing cost — not the iPhone. At base case, you spend Rp 35.3jt feeding free meals vs Rp 17.5jt on the phone. Track reward redemptions per outlet monthly and set a monthly food cost budget for the program separately from regular ops COGS.
✓
Set iPhone to 0 to size any other prize. The "prize budget available" line tells you exactly how much the program generates — use it to price a cash prize, Samsung phone, travel voucher, or split prizes across outlets. The loyalty economics are the same regardless of what you put in the winner's hands.
▲
Lucky draw entry controls. Require name + phone number written on each completed card. One entry per completed card, one winner. Lock submitted cards in a sealed box at each outlet. Announce draw date on the card design at launch — transparency drives trust and participation.
